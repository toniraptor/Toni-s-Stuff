All-kill
A song receives an “all-kill” distinction when it simultaneously reaches the #1 spot on all Korean music charts. PAK is Perfect All Kill, CAK is Certified All Kill and RAK is Real Time All Kill.

Maknae 막내 mak·nae
The youngest member of an idol group who is often the life of the party.They tend to be protected by the rest of the group and get away with being cheeky
Bias
Your favorite member in a group, the idol you’re biased toward
Ult/Ultimate bias
The one idol you love most compared to all other idols
Bias wrecker
An idol you have a fondness for, but is not your bias in your bias’ group
Fan chant
Words that are chanted by the fans in the audience during a performance to cheer them on. It often takes the form of the names of the singers and is usually shouted during parts of a song where the idols are not singing.
Cheol Sae Fan철새팬 noun:multifan
"철새" cheol sae means “migratory bird”, so when used in conjunction with “fan”, it refers to someone who is a fan of multiple groups at the same time. "다팬" da fan, also meaning “multi fan” can be used interchangeably.
Age Line
The term "line" can be translated as "grouping" or "belonging", and when combined with the term "age", it means in K-Pop terms people who were born in the same year within a group. It is normally abbreviated to the last two digits of the year, rather than their actual age. For example, the 93 liners would refer to members of a group born in 1993.
Aegyo애교 ae.gyo
Acting cutesy. Its a display of affection through various expressions such as making cute gestures or speaking in baby talk. It is exhibited by female a male K-Pop groups alike
Koreaboo한국어로
Non-Korean person who is strongly obsessed with Korean popular culture (want to be a Korean) but tends to get Korean words and facts wrong
Project group
A temporary group, ususally formed from a survival TV show (such as PD101 or The Unit) or made of idols from other groups (such as Cosmic Girls and Weki Meki forming WJMK)
Sajaegi 사재기 sah·jeh·kee
A method of manipulating chart rankings by a group’s own agency bulk-buying their albums. Literally “stockpiling”
Sasaeng 사생 sah·seng
“Stalker fan, not in a cute “stan” way. 사 sah “private>” 생 saeng “life” — referencing the fan’s invasion of an idol’s private life
Stan
A compound word made up of "stalker" and "fan". It describes an avid and obsessing fan. While the term carries some negative connotations, it is not as bad as saseng
